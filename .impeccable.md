## Design Context

### Users

US small CPA firms (1–3 CPAs · 20–300 clients · 2–10 states). Decision-makers are firm owners, partners, managers, preparers. The onboarding moment specifically: importing their existing client list from CCH Axcess / Drake / Lacerte / ProConnect / ProSeries / QuickBooks / TaxDome / UltraTax / Karbon / File In Time into DueDateHQ for the first time.

They arrive at this moment after deciding to switch tools — committed but skeptical, time-poor, and stressed. Tax-season pressure is the baseline emotion. They need to trust that the new tool understands their world before they commit hours of data entry.

### Brand Personality

**Calm · capable · sharp.**

Reassuring authority. Quiet confidence — like meeting a senior partner who's seen every tax season. We settle the user. We don't perform.

The interface speaks like a 15-year-veteran tax professional, NOT like:

- A cheeky consumer fintech app ("Yay, we found your file!")
- An enthusiastic AI assistant ("✨ Magic happens here!")
- A corporate enterprise tool ("Please configure your import preferences")
- A startup template ("Welcome aboard 🚀")

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visually consistent with the existing design system** (Tailwind tokens, the polished Stepper visual language, light-mode dominant). Do NOT introduce new fonts, new color palettes, or experimental visual directions.

**Boldness lives in INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE and INTERACTION, not in typography/color.** The brave moves are:

- Drastically reduce on-screen choices per step
- Surface AI intelligence as a structural hero, not as a banner footnote
- Single-affordance focus per state (one big thing, not five competing zones)
- Confident copy — sharp and professional, never cheeky, never apologetic

### Anti-references (all four)

- **Generic SaaS dashboard** — Inter, soft shadows on rounded rects, blue-primary, sterile equal-sized cards
- **AI-product cliches** — purple-blue gradients, sparkle icons, glassmorphism, animated mesh backgrounds, "magic" copy
- **Consumer fintech cheer** — cartoon illustrations, oversized rounded buttons, smiley microcopy, gift-box reward icons
- **Enterprise tax software** — wall-of-buttons toolbars, dense forms with no hierarchy, beige-on-beige

### Design Principles

1. **Reduce, then reveal.** Show one thing at a time. Trust the user to ask for more if they need it. The bold move is what you DON'T show.
2. **The AI is a senior partner, not an assistant.** When it makes a determination, it states the fact ("Drake export · 30 clients · 12 states"). It does not narrate the process ("Analyzing your file…").
3. **Numbers as protagonists.** Real counts, sharp typography, large but quiet. Replace prose with stats wherever possible.
4. **Calm motion.** Step transitions use confident ease-out (cubic-bezier, ~250-350ms). Detection lands like a typewriter strike — single, definitive. No spinning sparkles, no confetti, no bounce.
5. **Stay in the design system.** Same tokens, same primitives, same visual language as the rest of the app. The boldness is structural, not decorative.
