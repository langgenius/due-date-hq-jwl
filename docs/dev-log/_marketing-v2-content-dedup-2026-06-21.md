# Marketing v2 — content de-duplication pass

**Date:** 2026-06-21 · `docs/marketing/design-explorations/production-v2.html`. Acting on a background content audit (`/critique` of the copy). Principle applied: **each claim gets one rightful owner; everywhere else it's demoted or reworded to say something new** — demote, don't delete.

## Edits

- **Product-surfaces cards** (the worst offenders — each quoted the section it links to almost verbatim). Reworded all four to _navigation_ copy that teases the surface's own angle:
  - Alerts → "The live feed — every change as it lands, sorted by severity."
  - Coverage → "One map of every jurisdiction — see where changes are landing." (was a verbatim dupe of the sources section)
  - Worklist → "Your clients in risk order — who to call first, at a glance." (was word-for-word the `#work` heading)
  - Apply & audit → "Push a change to everyone it touches — with the full trail behind it." (dropped the "sourced, reversible, audited" triplet that the thumbnail + trust band already carry)
- **How-it-works Watch node** — dropped the "IRS, all 50 states, and FEMA" enumeration (the `sources` section owns coverage); now "Official federal and state sources…", keeping the grounding novelty.
- **How-it-works Apply node** — dropped "sourced, audited, undo within 24h" (the trust band owns reversibility + audit); kept the approval gate, which is what the Apply step is actually about.
- **Notice "Who's affected" caption** — "+ 9 more matched by jurisdiction, form & entity type" was verbatim the Match node; → "+ 9 more on the same form & entity type" (example-specific).
- **Trust band** — the sourcing formula ("a link, the exact quote, a verified date, or it's held back") was stated twice within the section. Kept it in the lead; simplified the 100%/Sourced stat to "Nothing is shown without a citation back to the official text."
- **FAQ Q3** — was the formula's third statement. Repurposed around the genuinely-new line: leads with "**It never invents a date.**"
- **Trust boundary** — removed "your data stays in your own isolated practice, never pooled across firms" (the security section's "Yours alone" owns data isolation); boundary now focuses purely on the not-tax-advice disclaimer.
- **Footer** — "FED + 50 states + DC" appeared twice; replaced the bottom-bar repeat with "Your data stays in your practice."
