Tax Preparation

7 tools

Professional tax preparation software prepares, reviews, and e-files federal and state returns for a firm's individual and business clients. Unlike consumer apps, it handles every entity return (1040, 1120, 1120-S, 1065, 1041, 990), multi-state filings, and K-1s, runs on a preparer-and-reviewer workflow, and carries client data forward each year. It's the engine a US tax practice is built on — and usually its largest software decision.

How we define this category

To qualify for inclusion in Tax Preparation, a product must:

  • Prepare and calculate US federal and state income tax returns for multiple entity types
  • Support IRS Modernized e-File (MeF) and the corresponding state e-filing
  • Carry client and return data forward between tax years
  • Provide distinct preparer and reviewer steps for in-firm review
  • Maintain a roster of clients with their entity and return types

Deadlines & Monitoring

3 tools

These tools make sure no client filing slips. Passive trackers record the due dates you enter and roll them forward each period. Active monitors also watch official sources — the IRS, state tax agencies, and FEMA — and flag when a date or rule changes and which clients it hits. In most firms this lives inside practice management; the specialists below focus on it.

How we define this category

To qualify for inclusion in Deadline & Compliance Tracking, a product must:

  • Track statutory filing due dates and extensions for a firm's clients
  • Roll recurring deadlines forward across periods and tax years
  • Alert or remind the firm ahead of upcoming due dates
  • (Active monitors) detect changes to official filing dates or rules and identify the affected clients

Practice Management

10 tools

Practice management software runs a firm's front and back office: service catalog, workflows and tasks, client records and communication, deadlines, and billable time — one place to see who's doing what, for which client, by when. Best for solo-to-mid firms leaving spreadsheets and email behind. Deadline tracking is a feature here, not a separate category.

How we define this category

To qualify for inclusion in Practice Management, a product must:

  • Maintain the catalog of services the firm offers
  • Manage relationships with clients and prospects
  • Track tasks, deadlines, and activities across the practice
  • Track the status of each engagement and capture billable time
  • Provide a portal or secure channel for client communication
  • Generate invoices for services and track payment

Accounting & Bookkeeping

5 tools

The general ledger records a business's transactions — bank feeds, invoicing, bills, reporting. For a firm it's usually the client's book of record, used for write-up, review, and close. QuickBooks Online and Xero dominate US small business, and most firm tools connect back to them — which makes this layer the hub of a firm's integrations.

How we define this category

To qualify for inclusion in Accounting & Bookkeeping, a product must:

  • Record and categorize a business's financial transactions in a general ledger
  • Support bank and credit-card feeds and reconciliation
  • Produce core financial statements (profit & loss, balance sheet)
  • Provide an accountant or firm view into client books
  • Offer invoicing and/or accounts-payable functions

Frequently asked questions

What is the best tax preparation software for a small CPA firm?

It depends on volume and return complexity. Drake Tax leads among sole practitioners on price and speed; Intuit Lacerte and ProConnect suit small firms with more complex returns; Thomson Reuters UltraTax CS and Wolters Kluwer CCH Axcess dominate at mid-to-large firms. (2025 AICPA tax software survey.)

Which CPA practice management software has an open API?

Karbon and TaxDome offer near-self-serve APIs with webhooks, and Financial Cents exposes an open API on higher plans. Canopy's API is approval-gated. Keeper, Firm360, and Pixie integrate mainly through Zapier rather than a direct API.

What is the difference between a deadline tracker and a compliance monitor?

A passive tracker such as File In Time records the due dates you enter and rolls them forward each year. An active monitor such as DueDateHQ additionally watches official sources — the IRS, state tax agencies, and FEMA — and flags when a filing date changes and which of your clients it affects.

How is CPA Field Guide different from G2 or Capterra?

It is editorial and vendor-neutral. No vendor pays for inclusion, placement, or ranking, and there is no sponsored sort. Every tool is described only by capabilities it has shipped, sourced from the vendor's own documentation, and organized around US tax-practice workflows.

Which accounting software integrates with QuickBooks Online?

QuickBooks Online offers a self-serve public API and an app store, so most firm tools connect to it — including practice-management platforms like Karbon and Canopy and bookkeeping-workflow tools like Keeper, which offers a two-way sync. Xero provides a comparable open API.

What tax software do the largest US accounting firms use?

At the largest firms, Thomson Reuters UltraTax CS and Wolters Kluwer CCH Axcess Tax lead, per the 2025 AICPA survey — both are suite or cloud systems with deeper integration and e-file capacity than the desktop products favored by smaller shops.
