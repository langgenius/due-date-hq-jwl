Questions

The short answers.

How is this different from File In Time?
File In Time records the deadlines you enter. DueDateHQ watches them for changes — and the moment the IRS or a state moves one, shows you which clients it affects, with the official source attached. Monitoring, not a deadline table.
Do I have to leave TaxDome / Karbon / Canopy?
No. It's deliberately narrow — the weekly deadline-triage layer, email-first with no portal to configure, so it sits alongside whatever you already run.
How do I know the dates are right?
Every date keeps its IRS or state source link, an exact quote, and a verified date — and anything that can't be grounded in the source is held back, not shown. It never invents a date.
Be ready before busy season

Walk into next busy season already ahead.

Paste your clients, and a sourced year of deadlines is ready in about 30 minutes. From there, DueDateHQ watches every change — so the next time a state moves a deadline, you'll already know who it affects.
