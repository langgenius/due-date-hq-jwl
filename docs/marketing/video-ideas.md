# DueDateHQ — Video Ideas (brainstorm)

**Status:** Video concept brainstorm for launch + ongoing content · 2026-06-18. Grounded in the locked positioning (`landing-page-copy.md`) and the real product loop (`unique-selling-points.md`). Pairs with the landing/marketing doc set.

---

## North star (every video obeys these)

- **Sell the *after*, prove the *loop*.** The feeling we sell is *calm / ahead / never blindsided*. The proof is the loop: it watches → flags who's affected → you fix in one click, sourced.
- **Show the real product.** Screen-capture the actual app wherever possible. The product *is* the demo.
- **Honesty (same rules as the page):** no fabricated testimonials, no fake customer counts, no "AI" voiceover crutch (say "it reads / it flags / it watches"), no "Radar," no pricing on awareness videos. Source-and-trust claims stay literally true.
- **Signature visual motif:** the **document → extraction** — an official notice (an IRS / "Texas Comptroller" release) styled like a real document, then the deadline change + affected clients lift out of it into clean labeled fields. It's literally what the product does, and it's the most ownable image we have. Reuse it across films, explainers, and bumpers.

---

## The 4 to make first (recommended launch slate)

1. **"The not-knowing"** — 60s emotional hero film (homepage + paid social).
2. **"Watch a change get caught"** — 45s product demo, screen capture (site how-it-works + YouTube).
3. **"We read the notice for you"** — 30s document→extraction explainer (the "Why you can trust it" section).
4. **3× 6-second bumpers** — pre-roll / paid social retargeting.

These four cover the funnel (emotion → product → trust → retargeting), share one shoot/asset set, and reuse the document-extraction motif.

## Full concept list

> Each: **format · length · funnel stage · the hook (first 3s) · beats · CTA · where · effort.**

### 1 · "The not-knowing" — emotional hero film
- **Format:** cinematic b-roll + VO · **Length:** 60–75s · **Stage:** awareness/brand · **Effort:** high
- **Hook (0–3s):** a CPA at a dark desk, 2 a.m., dozens of browser tabs glowing. VO: *"It's not missing a deadline that keeps you up."*
- **Beats:** the villain beat made real — fifty state tabs, an overflowing inbox, a spreadsheet only they understand → *"It's not knowing if you did."* → cut to morning: an Alert is already waiting, "12 clients affected" already flagged, one click → the CPA closes the laptop, calm. VO: *"DueDateHQ watches every change, so you always know."*
- **CTA:** *Catch every change. → duedatehq.com* · **Where:** homepage hero, paid social, LinkedIn.

### 2 · "Watch a tax-deadline change get caught" — product demo
- **Format:** clean screen capture + captions (no VO needed) · **Length:** 40–60s · **Stage:** consideration · **Effort:** med
- **Hook (0–3s):** a state-agency page posts a relief postponement → a DueDateHQ Alert slides in: *"Texas raised the franchise-tax threshold."*
- **Beats:** Alert → "12 clients affected · 9 eligible · 3 need review" → open → the affected-client list → hover the source (the exact quote from the notice) → **Apply to 12** → resolved, "undo within 24h." All real UI, calm pacing, mono captions.
- **CTA:** *Try it live →* · **Where:** site "how it works", YouTube, sales follow-up.

### 3 · "We read the notice for you" — document→extraction explainer
- **Format:** motion graphics (the signature motif) · **Length:** 25–35s · **Stage:** consideration/trust · **Effort:** med
- **Hook (0–3s):** a real-looking IRS/state notice fills the frame; a highlight sweeps the key line.
- **Beats:** the notice's key sentence highlights → lifts out into labeled fields (*the change* · *new date* · *who's affected* · *source quote*) → the fields snap into the Alert card → a small "source" link stays attached. VO/caption: *"Every date traces back to the official notice it came from."*
- **CTA:** *See how it works →* · **Where:** the "Why you can trust it" section, explainer/educational.

### 4 · 6-second bumpers (×3) — pre-roll
- **Format:** kinetic type + one product frame · **Length:** 6s each · **Stage:** retargeting/awareness · **Effort:** low
- **A:** *"A rule changed. You already knew."* (Alert frame)
- **B:** *"Who's affected? Already flagged."* (affected-clients frame)
- **C:** *"Fix 12 clients in one click."* (Apply → undo frame)
- **CTA:** logo + *duedatehq.com* · **Where:** YouTube bumpers, paid social.

### 5 · "The 5 a.m. rule change" — split-screen micro-story
- **Format:** split-screen narrative · **Length:** 20–30s · **Stage:** awareness · **Effort:** med
- **Hook:** "5:00 a.m. — a state moves a deadline." Two CPAs, split screen.
- **Beats:** left CPA finds out weeks later from an angry client; right CPA got the Alert at 5:01, already handled it. End on the contrast.
- **CTA:** *Be the one who already knew.* · **Where:** paid social, LinkedIn.

### 6 · "Excel vs DueDateHQ — same Monday" — comparison
- **Format:** parallel screen capture, light humor · **Length:** 30–45s · **Stage:** consideration · **Effort:** med
- **Hook:** "Two CPAs. Same Monday. Same 80 clients."
- **Beats:** one in spreadsheet-and-inbox hell (manual sorting, second-guessing); the other opens a ranked queue, five clients up top, done in five minutes. Use the comparison-table verdicts (watches sources / flags who's affected / one-click fix) as on-screen ticks.
- **CTA:** *See the comparison →* · **Where:** the File-In-Time/compare page, paid.

### 7 · Founder explainer — "why we built this"
- **Format:** talking head + b-roll · **Length:** 90s–2min · **Stage:** trust/About · **Effort:** med
- **Hook:** founder, direct to camera: "Every CPA I know has a story about the deadline that almost slipped."
- **Beats:** the origin (the chaos of 50 state sites), the principle (*every date shows its source; nothing changes without you*), what it does. Honest, plain, no hype.
- **CTA:** *Open the workbench →* · **Where:** About/Security page, LinkedIn, sales decks.

### 8 · "Set up in 30 minutes" — onboarding loom
- **Format:** narrated screen capture · **Length:** 2–3min · **Stage:** activation · **Effort:** low
- **Hook:** "Paste your client list. Here's everything that happens next."
- **Beats:** paste/import → a sourced year of deadlines appears → the first ranked Monday queue → the first Alert. Real, unhurried.
- **CTA:** *Start free →* · **Where:** in-product onboarding, trial emails, sales follow-up.

### 9 · "Deadline of the week" — recurring LinkedIn series
- **Format:** founder/expert talking head, 30–60s, weekly · **Stage:** organic/authority · **Effort:** low (recurring)
- **Hook:** "This week, [State] moved [deadline]. Here's who it hits."
- **Beats:** break down one real recent change + which entity types/clients it affects + what to check. Ends with the product catching it. Builds the content engine + SEO/social presence; positions DueDateHQ as the people who actually watch.
- **CTA:** soft — *We watch all 50 states so you don't.* · **Where:** LinkedIn, X, email digest.

### 10 · "Watch · Match · Apply" — animated loop
- **Format:** looping kinetic motion graphic (no people) · **Length:** 12–20s · **Stage:** awareness/hero · **Effort:** low–med
- **Hook:** the three words animate in sequence with one product frame each.
- **Beats:** Watch (sources scanning) → Match (clients light up) → Apply (one click, undo timer). Seamless loop; works muted.
- **CTA:** logo end-card · **Where:** hero background, social, conference loop.

### 11 · "Sources we watch" — montage bumper
- **Format:** fast-cut montage · **Length:** 15s · **Stage:** awareness · **Effort:** low
- **Hook:** rapid cuts of official source pages (IRS, FTB, DTF, Comptroller, DOR, FEMA) being scanned.
- **Beats:** the scan accelerates across agencies → resolves to *"We watch all 50 states. So you don't."*
- **CTA:** logo · **Where:** pre-roll, top-of-funnel.

### 12 · "Busy season, already ahead" — seasonal spot
- **Format:** b-roll + VO, seasonal · **Length:** 30s · **Stage:** awareness, timed to season · **Effort:** med
- **Hook:** "Busy season is coming. You can meet it two ways."
- **Beats:** the dread version vs. the calm-and-ahead version (the close's promise) → tie to the season timing.
- **CTA:** *Walk into busy season already ahead. →* · **Where:** seasonal paid, email.

## Production guardrails (don't violate)

- **No fabricated proof.** No invented testimonials, headshots, firm names, or "trusted by N firms." Use the real product, the real sources, and honest principle lines. (Real testimonials slot in the moment they exist.)
- **No "AI" as the hero word**; attribute to the product ("it reads the notice," "it flags the clients"). No "Radar." Alert product = "Alerts."
- **Bold on scope/speed** ("all 50 states," "24/7," "in one click") is fine; **source/trust claims stay literally true** ("every date traces to its official source," "you approve every change," "undo within 24h"). No auto-file / tax-advice implications.
- **Demo data is sample data** — label it "sample / not your data," same as the site.
- **Captions-first.** Most of these run muted on social — design for sound-off (captions + on-screen text), sound-on as enhancement.
